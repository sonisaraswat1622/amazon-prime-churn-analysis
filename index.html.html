The Problem

A contaminated north star metric

When Amazon introduced ads to Prime Video in January 2024, subscriber churn hit its highest rate since Kantar began tracking. By Q2, aggregate numbers recovered. Leadership declared success.

But a quiet problem remained: nearly 38% of Q1 churns produced no clean behavioral signal — no ad fatigue trace, no content abandonment pattern, no billing failure. They simply disappeared.

The reason: Amazon's single "churn rate" KPI is blending five fundamentally different subscriber populations with completely different churn drivers. Blend them into one number and you get a dashboard that looks healthy while real engagement decay compounds underneath.

Proposed Framework

Content-Signal Weighted Churn (CSWC)

Standard churn rate = total churns / total subscribers. The problem: it treats all subscriber segments as equivalent and buries dark churn in an aggregate that looks healthy.

The fix: weight each segment's churn contribution by its attribution confidence — the probability that a churn event has an identifiable behavioral signal.

CSWC = Σ(segment_churn_rate × attribution_confidence_i)
         ────────────────────────────────────────────────
                 Σ(attribution_confidence_i)

This forces dark churn to surface as a first-class metric rather than disappear into an average. The recommendation to leadership: don't report a single churn KPI — report a confidence interval, and make the dark churn bucket something product, content, and pricing teams review monthly.

TIER 1 — Hard Signal
Trigger: Ad frequency >12/hr + 7-day inactivity, or explicit cancel survey citing ads.  Action: Cap ad load immediately.
TIER 2 — Soft Signal
Trigger: Watch time declining >25% in 30 days pre-cancel, no hard threshold crossed.  Action: Content investment signal review.
TIER 3 — Dark Churn
Trigger: No behavioral degradation before cancel.  Action: Credential-sharing audit; seasonal re-subscription pattern analysis.

Chart 5 — Cohort Recovery

Recovery varied significantly by market

Recovery index: 100 = January 2024 launch baseline. Each line shows how the launch cohort's subscriber count evolved per market over 6 months.

The UK market saw the steepest initial drop (to index 68) but recovered steadily. Germany recovered slowest — suggesting pricing sensitivity and content library gaps are more influential outside English-speaking markets. A single global policy rollout misses these dynamics entirely.

  • US: Fastest recovery — bundle protection and broader content library cushioned the impact
  • UK: Steepest drop, steady recovery — ad-free tier was well-communicated post-launch
  • DE: Slowest recovery — localized content gaps + price sensitivity require regional strategy
